Frequently Asked Questions

Common Questions

Everything you need to know about our audio production software and tools.

What is an AAF file and why would I need to convert it to Ableton Live?

AAF (Advanced Authoring Format) is the industry-standard file format used by professional video editors and audio engineers to exchange timeline data between digital audio workstations and non-linear editors. If you receive a project from Pro Tools, Premiere Pro, DaVinci Resolve, Media Composer, or Adobe Audition, Abletonlive.aaf lets you import that entire timeline into Ableton Live with sample-accurate precision -- preserving clip placement, volume automation, fades, and crossfades.

Which DAWs and video editors are compatible with Abletonlive.aaf?

Abletonlive.aaf works with any application that exports standard AAF files. This includes Avid Pro Tools, Adobe Premiere Pro, Blackmagic DaVinci Resolve, Avid Media Composer, Adobe Audition, and most other professional NLE and DAW software. The converted session opens directly in Ableton Live 10, 11, or 12.

How does ABLETUBE stream YouTube audio into Ableton Live?

ABLETUBE uses a Node.js Electron server that captures audio from YouTube and streams it in real-time to a Max for Live device inside Ableton Live using the Audio over OSC protocol. This creates a low-latency audio bridge between web content and your DAW, allowing you to sample, remix, record, and process any YouTube audio directly in your session.
