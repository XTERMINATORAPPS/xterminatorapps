Import Premiere Pro Audio into Ableton Live

Published April 2026
Read time 6 min
Category Workflow
Sections 4
Article · Workflow · 6 min

Video editors working in Adobe Premiere Pro often need to hand off audio to a music producer or sound designer who works in Ableton Live. The standard approach -- bouncing a stereo mix or individual stems -- destroys the timeline structure and wastes hours of work.

A better method uses AAF export from Premiere Pro to preserve the complete multitrack timeline, then converts it directly into an Ableton Live session.

Why Premiere Pro to Ableton?

Premiere Pro is excellent for video editing but limited for advanced audio processing. Producers who receive video projects often need to:

  • Replace or enhance the music score
  • Apply professional audio effects and mastering
  • Remix or rearrange the audio for different cuts
  • Add sound design elements using Ableton instruments
  • Collaborate with musicians who work exclusively in Ableton

How to Export AAF from Premiere Pro

  1. Open your project in Adobe Premiere Pro
  2. Select the sequence you want to export
  3. Go to File > Export > AAF
  4. Choose your audio format settings (WAV recommended)
  5. Select a destination folder
  6. Click OK to export the AAF file with embedded or referenced media

Converting to Ableton Live

Open Abletonlive.aaf and select the exported AAF file. The converter reconstructs the entire Premiere Pro audio timeline as an Ableton Live Set with all clips placed at their exact positions, including fades and volume changes.

Tips for Best Results

  • Consolidate your Premiere Pro timeline before exporting to avoid referencing missing media
  • Use WAV format for the highest audio quality during export
  • Name your tracks clearly in Premiere Pro -- these names carry over to Ableton
  • Export at the same sample rate your Ableton project will use
