Free vs Paid DAW Converter Tools Compared

April 2026 -- 7 min read

When you need to move a project between DAWs, you have two options: spend time doing it manually for free, or invest in a dedicated converter tool. This comparison breaks down what each approach actually costs in time and quality.

The "Free" Method: Manual Stem Export

The most common free approach is bouncing stems from one DAW and importing them into another. Here is what that involves:

  • Solo each track and bounce individually -- 2 to 5 minutes per track
  • Import all stems into the destination DAW
  • Manually align each stem to the correct timeline position
  • Recreate volume automation from scratch
  • Rebuild fades and crossfades manually

For a 20-track session, this process takes 1 to 3 hours of tedious manual work. And you permanently lose individual clip boundaries.

The Paid Method: Dedicated Converter

A dedicated converter like Abletonlive.aaf reads the source project file (AAF) and generates a complete session in the destination DAW format. The process takes under 30 seconds regardless of session complexity.

What You Get

  • Sample-accurate clip placement
  • Volume automation preserved
  • Fades and crossfades intact
  • Clip gain values maintained
  • Track names and organization kept
  • Marker positions transferred

The Real Cost Comparison

At a professional hourly rate of $50 to $150, a single manual session transfer costs more in time than the one-time purchase price of most converter tools. If you transfer projects regularly, the paid tool pays for itself on the first use.

When Free Makes Sense

If you transfer sessions once a year and the project has fewer than 5 tracks, manual export is reasonable. For everything else, a dedicated tool saves significant time and delivers better results.
